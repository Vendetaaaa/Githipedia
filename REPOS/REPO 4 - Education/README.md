## What Is GitHub Education?

GitHub Education gives students and teachers free access to professional developer tools that would otherwise cost a few hundred dollars a year. The main thing is the **Student Developer Pack**, a bundle of 100+ real tools used by working developers, all free for as long as you're in school.

It's been running since 2013 and has helped over 6 million students and teachers at this point. It's not a trial and it's not a watered-down version. It's the actual products.

There are two tracks:

- **Students** get the Developer Pack, Copilot, and GitHub Pro, all free
- **Teachers/Educators** get GitHub Team for organizations, Classroom tools, and teaching resources

## Who Can Join?

### 🧑‍🎓 Students

You qualify if all three of these are true:

| Requirement | Details |
|---|---|
| **Currently enrolled** | At an accredited institution - university, college, bootcamp, high school |
| **Age** | 13 or older |
| **Have proof** | A school email address OR official enrollment documentation |

You don't need to be studying computer science. Any student at any accredited institution qualifies; business, medicine, arts, engineering, whatever.

The key word is *currently*. Graduated students aren't eligible, even if they graduated last week. Apply before you lose access to your school email.

### 👩‍🏫 Teachers

You qualify if you're a current teacher, professor, or instructor at an accredited institution, primary, secondary, or higher education. Coding bootcamp instructors are also eligible.

GitHub's definition of "educator" is fairly broad. If you're teaching programming or tech-adjacent subjects in a formal setting, you're almost certainly in.

## How to Apply - Step by Step

The application is at **[education.github.com/discount_requests/application](https://education.github.com/discount_requests/application)**.

Before you start, have one of these ready:
- Your **school-issued email** (e.g. `you@university.edu`)
- **OR** a document proving enrollment: transcript, student ID, acceptance letter, tuition receipt

**1.** Sign into your GitHub account. Create one if you don't have it.

**2.** Add your school email to your account:

```
Settings → Emails → Add email address → enter your .edu email → verify it
```

**3.** Go to the application, click "Get student benefits."

**4.** Select "Student."

**5.** Select your school (type it in if it doesn't appear).

**6.** Upload proof of enrollment if prompted.

**7.** Submit. Most applications get reviewed within a few days, sometimes hours.

You'll get an email when it's reviewed. Check spam if you don't hear back within a week.

## Verification - What GitHub Actually Checks

GitHub uses a two-path system depending on what you provide.

### Path 1: School Email (fastest)

If your school email ends in `.edu` (US) or another recognized academic domain, GitHub can often auto-verify you without needing any documents. This can happen in minutes.

To maximize your shot at auto-approval:
- Add and verify your `.edu` email to your GitHub account *before* applying
- Select that email when you apply
- Make sure your GitHub account has a real profile photo and bio; new, empty-looking accounts sometimes get flagged

### Path 2: Document Upload (manual review)

If you don't have a school email or it can't be auto-verified, you'll be asked to upload proof of enrollment.

**What works:**

| Document | Requirements |
|---|---|
| Student ID | Must show name, institution, current academic year |
| Official transcript | Current semester, shows your name and school |
| Enrollment letter | From registrar or admissions |
| Tuition receipt | Dated, showing current enrollment |
| Report card | For high school students |

**Tips:**
- The date needs to be current. A transcript from two semesters ago often gets rejected
- Upload a clear, unobstructed photo or scan. Blurry images get declined automatically
- During peak times (September and January when semesters start) manual review can take 1-2 weeks

## When It Gets Rejected and What to Do

Getting rejected is frustrating, but it's usually fixable. Here are the most common reasons:

**"We couldn't verify your academic affiliation"**
Your school email wasn't recognized. Try uploading a document instead; go back to your application and resubmit with documentation.

**"Your proof of enrollment appears outdated"**
Upload something more recent. A current semester transcript or an enrollment verification letter from your registrar works best.

**"The image you uploaded was unclear"**
Retake the photo in decent lighting so all text is readable, and resubmit.

**"Your account doesn't appear to be a real user"**
GitHub flags very new or empty accounts. Add a profile photo, write a bio, make a few commits or star some repos, wait a few days, then reapply.

**Rejected with no clear reason?**
Contact GitHub Support at [support.github.com](https://support.github.com) directly. Explain you're a student and include your school name. Human review overturns automated rejections fairly often.

## Availability by Country

GitHub Education is available worldwide. There's no built-in country restriction.

A few things vary by region:

| What Varies | Details |
|---|---|
| **School email recognition** | US `.edu` addresses auto-verify. Other countries use different TLDs (`.ac.uk`, `.edu.au`, etc.); these also work but usually go to manual review |
| **Partner offer availability** | Some Pack partners restrict to certain countries; check each partner's page |

Countries where students have widely reported success include the US, UK, Canada, Australia, Germany, France, Brazil, India, South Korea, and most of the EU. If your institution is accredited in your country, you're eligible. It might just require document upload instead of email auto-verification.

## How Long Does It Last?

As long as your student status is active and verifiable.

```
Apply → Approved → Benefits active (typically 1-2 years)
                         ↓
              GitHub sends an expiry notice
                         ↓
              Reapply with updated proof → Renewed
```

GitHub emails you when access is about to expire. You can renew by reapplying with fresh proof of enrollment. After graduation, benefits end. You move to regular GitHub pricing and won't be charged anything for the lapse.

Set a reminder to renew 2 weeks before your expected expiry, especially during summer when you might not have access to your school email.

## Teachers: What's Different for You

Educators get their own set of benefits through the **GitHub Campus Program**.

**What educators get:**

- **GitHub Team** (normally $4/user/month) - free for your classroom organization
- **GitHub Classroom** - free tooling to assign, collect, and auto-grade coding assignments
- **Unlimited private repositories** for your organization
- **GitHub Codespaces** hours for students

**How to apply:**

Same application page. Select "Teacher." You'll verify with a school-issued faculty email or official employment documentation (faculty ID, staff appointment letter).

GitHub Classroom is worth setting up even if you don't care about the other benefits. Managing coding assignments across a class without it is genuinely painful.

## GitHub Campus Expert Program

A separate program for student leaders who want to build developer communities on their campus. This one isn't just about free tools; it's for people who are already active.

**What you get:**
- Official Campus Expert title and a platform to run events under GitHub's umbrella
- Training and workshops from GitHub staff
- GitHub swag and event funding
- Access to a global network of Campus Experts

**How to apply:**

Applications open periodically at [education.github.com/experts](https://education.github.com/experts). There's a written application and an interview stage. It's competitive and cohorts are small globally.

You'll want to already be involved in your campus tech scene before applying. This program is for people who are building community, not just looking for perks.
